about

I will be a second year PhD Student in the Management Science & Engineering (MS&E) Department at Stanford University beginning in the 2016-2017 academic year and am a member of MS&E’s Social Algorithms Lab (SOAL). My research focuses on social network analysis: modeling human behavior in digital systems and mining large-scale graph datasets to better understand how new technologies intersect with established social systems that feature complex cultural and political considerations.

I received my BS in Mathematics from Ohio University in 2008 where I was also a Barry M. Goldwater Scholar, completed a research fellowship at Stanford Law School in 2012-2014, and received my AM in Statistics from Harvard University in 2015. I am also a 2016 recipient of the National Defense Science & Engineering Graduate Fellowship.

ongoing projects

  • Ruffled Feathers: When Can Gender be Inferred on Social Networks? - Joint work with Johan Ugander
    • Talks: IPAM Culture Analytics Workshop on Cultural Patterns (Ugander: May 2016), International Conference on Computational Social Science (upcoming: June 2016), SIAM Workshop on Network Science (upcoming: July 2016)

  • Re(ef)source - Crowdsourced Coral Reef Imaging - Recipient of a Magic Grant - Brown Institute for Media Innovation (2016-2017). Joint collaboration between Stanford and Columbia University graduate students.
    • Explore the potential for combining new imaging modalities with crowdsourcing data collection efforts of coral reefs in order to provide a model for participatory storytelling and citizen journalism in environmental reporting.

  • Linking Women to Opportunity: Evaluating Gender Differences in Self-Promotion - Joint project with LinkedIn.
    • Examine differences in whether/how men and women self-promote online, and whether the effects of self-promotion on career opportunity vary by gender. On a broader level, we hope that our research not only empowers women to maximize career success, but also informs recruiters who are seeking gender equality within their organizations. Winner of LinkedIn's Economic Graph Challenge, May 2015. [link]
