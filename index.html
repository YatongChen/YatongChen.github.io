About

    I am a first year Ph.D. in the Computer Science and Engineering Department at the University of California, Santa Cruz. My research focuses on developing algorithmic and statistical frameworks for analyzing social networks, and other large-scale behavioral data. Specifically, I'm interested in the challenges of causal inference and fairness issue in these complex domains. My interests lies in the intersection of the intersections of machine learning, statistics, optimization, graph theory, and algorithm design.

    I received my BS in Energy and Resources Engineering and BA in Economics from Peking University in 2016. After that, I recieved a MS in Statistics at Stanford University, where I worked with Professor Johan Ugander on node classification problem using semi-supervised learning.
