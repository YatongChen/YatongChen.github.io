About Me

    I am a third year Ph.D. in the Computer Science and Engineering Department at the University of California, Santa Cruz, fortunate to be advised by Professor Yang Liu. My research focuses on the social aspect of machine learning, taking into account human decision subjects' responses when developing algorithmic systems.

    I received a MS in Statistics at Stanford University in 2019, where I worked with Professor Johan Ugander on node classification problem using semi-supervised learning. Before that, I received my BS in Energy and Resources Engineering and BA in Economics from Peking University in 2016.
